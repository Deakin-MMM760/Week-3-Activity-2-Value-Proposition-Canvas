Activity 2 | Value Proposition Canvas

Use this worksheet to draft a simple Value Proposition Canvas for one customer segment. Keep the focus on what the customer is trying to do, what frustrates them, what good looks like to them, and how your offer responds.

What you’re producing

One clearly chosen customer segment.

A customer profile with jobs, pains and gains.

A value map with products and services, pain relievers and gain creators.

One top pain and one top gain to focus on.

One proof question that helps test whether the pain is real and important.

1. Choose one customer segment

Pick one segment only. This worksheet works best when you are specific about who the business is really for.

Describe this segment in one clear sentence.

2. Customer profile

Think from the customer’s point of view. Describe what they are trying to get done, what gets in the way, and what positive outcomes they want.

Prompt

Customers are trying to get important things done, avoid frustrations, and achieve outcomes that matter to them.

Functional jobs

What practical tasks are they trying to complete?

Social jobs

How do they want to be seen by others?

Emotional jobs

How do they want to feel, or avoid feeling?

What annoys them, costs them time or money, creates stress, or creates risk?

What does good look like? What do they value? What would genuinely improve the experience?

3. Value map

Now move from the customer to the offer. Explain what the business provides and how that offer reduces pains and creates gains.

Prompt

A strong offer connects clearly to the pains and gains that matter most for this specific customer segment.

What does the business actually provide?

How does the offer reduce the most important pains?

How does the offer create the gains the customer values most?

4. Focus the canvas

Choose the most important pain and gain. If you cannot name one of each, the offer is probably still too broad.

Prompt

Which pain matters most right now, and which gain would most clearly improve the customer’s experience?

What would you need to observe to believe this pain really matters?

Quality check

Are the jobs, pains and gains written from the customer’s point of view rather than the business point of view?

Do the pain relievers and gain creators connect clearly to the chosen segment?

Could someone read this and understand why this offer fits this customer?
